Aurat Foundation

Established in 1986, Aurat Foundation is one of Pakistan's most prominent non-governmental organizations dedicated to women's empowerment and human rights. They work tirelessly to promote gender equality, advocate for women's rightful place in the workplace, and ensure protection from domestic violence. Their initiatives often involve policy advocacy, research, and community mobilization to bring about systemic change and foster a safer environment for women across the nation.

Helpline: +92 51 111 123 456

Website: www.af.org.pk

Head Office: Islamabad, Pakistan

UN Women Pakistan

UN Women Pakistan plays a crucial role in advancing gender equality and the empowerment of women and girls across the country. As part of the global United Nations entity, they work to implement international norms and standards for gender equality, support policy reform, and engage in extensive community outreach programs. Their efforts are closely aligned with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), focusing on areas like economic empowerment, ending violence against women, and increasing women's leadership and participation in all spheres of life.

Bedari

Bedari is a prominent national NGO committed to creating a society where women and children are safe, educated, and enjoy gender justice. They focus on a wide range of issues, including combating violence against women and children, promoting girls' education, and empowering marginalized communities. Bedari implements community-based programs, conducts advocacy, and provides support services to victims, aiming to bring about positive social change and protect vulnerable populations from various forms of exploitation and discrimination.

PAIMAN Alumni Trust

PAIMAN Alumni Trust is a unique female-led NGO in Pakistan, with a strong focus on peacebuilding, conflict transformation, and women's economic empowerment. They work across various regions, often in conflict-affected areas, to foster harmony and resilience within communities. Through training, dialogue, and livelihood programs, PAIMAN empowers women to become agents of change, contribute to local economies, and play a vital role in conflict resolution and promoting social cohesion.
